The Purdue Model Architecture

The Purdue Model is the industry standard for segmenting Industrial Control Systems (ICS) from corporate IT networks.

Level 4 & 5: Enterprise Network

The corporate layer where business functions like email and ERP systems reside. This is the most common entry point for cyber threats.

Level 3.5: Industrial DMZ (IDMZ)

A critical buffer zone that prevents direct communication between the enterprise and the production floor.

Level 3: Operations Systems

Site-wide control systems, including HMI (Human Machine Interfaces) and data historians.

Level 2: Control Systems

Local supervisory controls and engineering workstations that manage the physical processes.

Level 0-1: Field Zone

The physical equipment, including sensors, actuators, and PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers).

Implementation of IEC 62443: Zones & Conduits

Following IEC 62443 standards, each level above is treated as a separate Security Zone. Communication between these zones is strictly controlled through Conduits.

By implementing an IDMZ at Level 3.5, we create a conduit that ensures no "dual-homed" servers exist, effectively stopping a breach in the Enterprise network from reaching the Field Zone.
