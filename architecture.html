The Purdue Model Architecture

The Purdue Model is the industry standard for segmenting Industrial Control Systems (ICS) from the corporate network.

The 5 Levels of Security

Level 4 & 5: Enterprise Network
The corporate office environment where email, web servers, and business systems reside.
Level 3.5: DMZ (Demilitarized Zone)
The critical buffer zone where security systems and firewalls separate the plant from the office.
Level 3: Operations Systems
Systems that monitor and control the entire plant, such as HMI (Human Machine Interfaces).
Level 2: Control Systems
The "brains" of the operation, including PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers).
Level 0-1: Field Zone
The physical devices: sensors, motors, and valves that do the actual work.
